About

ReforgedZ

Where it started

Before survival games were a genre, an Arma 2 mod changed what people thought multiplayer could feel like. It dropped you on Chernarus with nothing. No tutorial, no waypoints, no safe zone. Just you, the map, and 40 other players doing whatever it took to stay alive. You didn't know if the guy coming over the hill was going to help you or shoot you in the face. That was the whole point.

Then Epoch showed up and added structure to the chaos. Basebuilding, traders, vehicles, an actual economy. Suddenly you were playing for 8 hours straight defending a base you spent three days constructing. It was clunky as hell and absolutely brilliant. Between those two mods, a lot of us got our best gaming memories.

When Arma Reforger launched with the Enfusion engine, nobody had seriously tried to bring that era back. The tools were there, the map was there. We started building. Not a port, not a copy, something new that captures the same feeling. That's what ReforgedZ is.

The Origin

Dean "Rocket" Hall

Before survival games were a genre, Hall was a New Zealand army officer on exchange with the Singapore Armed Forces. During a jungle survival exercise in Brunei, things went badly wrong. He ran out of food, dropped 25 kilograms, and ended up in hospital requiring surgery.

Lying in that hospital bed, he started experimenting with Arma 2, trying to build something that could simulate the psychological weight of real survival for military training. The army had no interest. So he added zombies, called it DayZ, and put it online.

He built the whole thing as an active soldier, in his own time. When the mod launched in early 2012, it hit a million players inside four months and sent Arma 2 sales through the roof. No tutorials. No quest markers. Permanent death. The tension that came from not knowing whether the stranger across the field was going to help or shoot you turned out to be something nobody had seen before. Every survival game that came after owes something to what Hall put together from a hospital bed.

Built for Enfusion

The zombie AI, loot economy, medical system, basebuilding, vehicles, questing, trader networks, humanity and factions. All of it written from scratch for Arma Reforger. Nothing carried over from other games, nothing half-ported. It's built for this engine and this map.

The Vision

A survival server where what you do actually matters. Player interaction that has stakes. Factions that change your experience. A world that pushes back. We're drawing on the best parts of Arma 2's modding era and building something that fits where Arma is now.
